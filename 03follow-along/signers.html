Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration, Jefferson was a prominent Virginian and later became the third President of the United States.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence from Massachusetts, Adams played a key role in the Continental Congress and later became the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Representing Pennsylvania, Franklin was a reowned statesman, inventor, and diplomat, highly respected both in America and abroad.
John Hancock
As the President of the Second Continental Congress, Hancock's bold and prominent signature is one of the most recognizable on the Declaration.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams and influential revolutionary leader from Massachusetts, Samuel Adams was instrumental in organizing resistance to British policies.
Richard Henry Lee
A Virginian who proposed the resolution for independence, his motion set the stage for drafting the Declaration.
Roger Sherman
Represnting Connecticut, Sherman was the only person to sign all four major founding documents of the U.S.: the Declarationof Independence, the Articles of Confederation, the Constitution, and the Articles of Association.
Robert Morris
A financier from Pennsylvania who played a critical role in funding the Revolutionary War, Morris later signed the Constitution as well.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
